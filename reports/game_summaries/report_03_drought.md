# Game Report: Scarcity and Solidarity
**ID:** BG-2026-003 | **Date:** 2026-05-16

## 🌵 Scenario Overview
This game explored a **Dry Season** scenario, exacerbated by a **Severe Drought** that caused the water table to drop by 5 meters. An **Uncertainty** card introduced **Incomplete Information**, meaning that data on private well extraction was missing, making it impossible to know exactly who was using the most water.

## 🛠️ Team Strategies

### Government Team (Team A)
The government proposed an **Aquifer Recharge Program**. Recognizing the drop in the water table, they focused on deep-well injection and technical solutions to replenish ground water. They offered a **Subsidy for Drip Irrigation** to encourage farmers to move away from water-intensive flood irrigation.

### Community Team (Team B)
Representing the local farmers, this team focused on **Community-Managed Rationing**. They proposed a solar-powered micro-irrigation cooperative. They were initially wary of government control over their wells but recognized the technical necessity of the recharge program.

## 💬 Key Interactions
*   **Scientific Consensus:** Both teams used their **Power Cards** early. The Community consulted an **Environmentalist** on low-water techniques, while the Government used a **Hydrologist** to map the aquifer. This "Expert-led" approach helped ground the conversation in reality.
*   **The Compromise:** During the exchange, a breakthrough occurred. The Community agreed to allow government monitoring of their solar pumps in exchange for a direct subsidy on drought-resistant seeds.
*   **Solidarity:** Despite the "Incomplete Information" uncertainty, the teams agreed to trust each other's self-reported usage data until better sensors could be installed.

## 🏁 Final Outcome
**Result:** 🤝 **RESOURCE_SHARING_AGREEMENT**

**Facilitator's Note:** An excellent example of resource management. The teams successfully navigated the "tragedy of the commons" by focusing on mutual survival. The agreement to share monitoring data despite the uncertainty card was a significant demonstration of trust-building, a core goal of the BekiGame project.
