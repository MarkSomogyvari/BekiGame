# Game Report: High-Conflict Flash Flood
**ID:** BG-2026-002 | **Date:** 2026-05-16

## 🚨 Scenario Overview
A high-stakes simulation that quickly spiraled into crisis. During a **Monsoon** start, a **Major Flood** event occurred at the 5-minute mark, breaching a critical embankment in **Sector 4**. The situation was further complicated by an **Uncertainty** card: a **Policy Change** that re-allocated emergency funds away from rural areas toward urban centers.

## 🛠️ Team Strategies

### Government Team (Team A)
Faced with limited funding, the Government prioritized **Critical Infrastructure**. Their plan focused on protecting the regional Power Plant and the National Highway. To do this, they proposed "managed flooding" of agricultural zones (polders), effectively sacrificing farmland to save infrastructure.

### Community Team (Team B)
The community team, representing local farmers and fishing villages, were outraged by the polder flooding plan. They proposed a **Decentralized Disaster Response** model, including mobile medical units and localized sandbagging, arguing that "infrastructure doesn't matter if the people are dead."

## 💬 Key Interactions
*   **Media Impact:** The Media Bureau broadcasted a breaking news alert regarding the breach, which created immediate panic and forced the Government to issue evacuation orders before their plan was ready.
*   **Expert Tension:** The Government consulted a **Hydrologist** (Power Card), but used the data to justify why Sector 4 *had* to be sacrificed, which the Community perceived as using science to mask a political choice.
*   **The Exchange:** Comments were sharp. The Community accused the Government of prioritizing "factories over families," while the Government dismissed community sandbagging as "dangerously insufficient."

## 🏁 Final Outcome
**Result:** ❌ **CONFLICT_UNRESOLVED**

**Facilitator's Note:** This session highlighted the "Winner-Takes-All" mentality that BekiGame aims to solve. Both teams retreated into their core interests. The Government prioritized macro-economic stability, while the Community prioritized immediate survival, with neither side willing to find a middle ground on fund allocation.
