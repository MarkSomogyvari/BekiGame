# BekiGame Alignment Report: Policy Brief Integration
**Date:** 2026-05-16
**Source Document:** *Beki policy brief (IIT Guwahati / DEMOTAPE Project)*

## 1. Executive Summary of Alignment
The BekiGame prototype demonstrates a high degree of structural and thematic alignment with the 2026 Beki Policy Brief. The game effectively captures the transboundary complexity, multi-stakeholder tensions, and hydroclimatic uncertainties identified as critical challenges in the Kurichhu-Manas-Beki river system.

---

## 2. Key Alignment Areas

### A. Stakeholder Representation
*   **Policy Brief:** Highlights the "governance gap" between central agencies, state governments, and local communities.
*   **Game Alignment:** The roles of **DECISION_MAKER** (Government) and **COMMUNITY_MEMBER** (Local) directly simulate this power dynamic. The inclusion of **MEDIA** and **EXPERT** roles addresses the brief's call for "transparent communication" and "science-based understanding."

### B. Hydroclimatic Stress & Uncertainty
*   **Policy Brief:** Points to "increasing rainfall variability," "emerging drought signals," and "extreme flows."
*   **Game Alignment:** The **Season**, **Extreme Event**, and **Uncertainty** card systems accurately model these stressors. The inclusion of "Rainfall Variability" and "Severe Drought" cards directly reflects the SPI-3 trends mentioned in the brief.

### C. Information Flow & Narratives
*   **Policy Brief:** Identifies "mistrust" regarding water releases and "delays in data dissemination" as major bottlenecks.
*   **Game Alignment:** The **Media Bureau**'s broadcast system and the **Exchange Phase** (cross-team feedback) are perfect tools for simulating and resolving these narrative conflicts.

---

## 3. Identified Gaps & Recommendations

While the current prototype is strong, the following refinements would deepen its alignment with the specific regional context of the Beki basin:

| Policy Brief Priority | Current Game State | Recommended Refinement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Erosion Risk** | Focus is largely on Floods/Drought. | **Add "Bank Erosion" Extreme Event card.** Erosion is cited as a "bigger worry than flood" in the brief. |
| **Land Tenure/Tenure Complexities** | Not explicitly modeled. | **Add "Tenure Dispute" Uncertainty card.** This would simulate the difficulty in compensation mentioned in the brief. |
| **Transboundary Dynamics** | Generic roles. | **Explicitly name the players** as "Bhutanese Hydropower Authority" vs "Assam Downstream Community" to heighten the transboundary realism. |
| **Erosion Compensation** | General proposals. | **Add a rule/mechanism** where Team A must decide whether to recognize "Erosion" as a disaster category for compensation. |

---

## 4. Conclusion
BekiGame serves as a powerful "Basin-level coordination platform" in line with the brief's recommendation for a **Beki River Symposium**. It provides a safe, simulated environment to "depoliticise and decentralise hydrological data," fostering the dialogue necessary for long-term resilience in the Beki river basin.

**Action Plan:** I recommend adding a dedicated "Erosion & Tenure" card pack in the next development sprint to address the most critical long-term challenges identified by field evidence.
